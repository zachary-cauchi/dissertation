Breakdown of the question-answer-rationale annotations, and the image-accompanying metadata.

The following notes are to be considered:
* All answer and rationale choices are a correct choice in another question, but can be reused.
* Image metadata consists of [Detectron](https://github.com/facebookresearch/Detectron)-identified objects with bounding boxes and segments.
* Answers are ranked in descending correctness via Adversarial Matching scores:
  * 0 - Correct answer
  * 1 - 1st wrong answer
  * 2 - 2nd wrong answer
  * 3 - 3rd wrong answer.
* Images are ID'ed according to the split they belong to (train / val / test).
* question-answer-rationales are ID'ed separately for:
  1. The image they belong to.
  2. The set they belong to.
  3. The subset/fold they belong to.
* Test set does not contain the following keys:
  * interesting_scores
  * answer_likelihood
  * [answer|question|rationale]_orig
  * answer_match_iter
  * answer_sources
  * answer_label
  * rationale_match_iter
  * rationale_sources
  * rationale_label
* Tokenised representations of numbers are changed into single-token, multi-worded representations, and can contain human-induced irregularities to refer to the same number.
  * Eg: `2016` becomes `two thousand and sixteen point zero`, but could also be encountered as `two thousand and sixteen` or ` two thousand and sixteen point zero` with unnecessary spacing.
